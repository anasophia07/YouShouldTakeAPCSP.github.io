What is Computer Science?

Computer science (C.S.) is the study of computational systems and their functions, primarily dealing with how programs are made, what these programs are used for, and how they are designed. Overall, computer science allows individuals to turn large-scale problems into smaller ones that are easier to visualize.

Why take C.S. in high school?

Cognition

Taking computer science classes helps enhance problem-solving and organization skills

Job Opportunities

Developing computer literacy skills will help students adapt to the modern workforce that is moving towards technological innovation

Equity

Expanding computer science education to more schools gives more students easy access to computer resources

C.S. at West Shore

At West Shore, high school students can take AP CSP, which is an entry level class that requires no previous C.S. knowledge! If students desire to continue learning about computer science, they can take AP Computer Science A, that focuses on programming with Java!

CSP Students Learn About:

  • Programming (Python)
  • The Internet
  • Creative Development
  • Computing Systems
  • Impacts of Computing
